## FAQs

### Q: What does "Danger" really mean?
**A**: It means weather conditions are outside safe ranges for the crop. You should:
- Monitor closely
- Be ready to irrigate or drain water
- Protect plants if possible
- Consult a local agricultural expert

### Q: Can I rely on this 100%?
**A**: This is a helpful guide, but:
- Always observe your own fields
- Combine with local expert advice
- Consider your soil and water availability
- Adjust based on your experience

### Q: Why are my crops showing danger when it seems fine?
**A**: Weather can be deceiving:
- Overnight temperatures might drop below safe levels
- High wind can increase evaporation
- High humidity increases disease risk
- The system is being conservative to protect your crops

### Q: How often should I check?
**A**: It depends:
- Daily during critical growth phases
- Before irrigation or spraying
- When weather is changing
- During monsoon season

### Q: What if my city isn't available?
**A**: Try:
- Searching with just the city name (no district/state)
- Using the nearest major city
- Using state capital city for regional weather
- Contacting us with the city name
